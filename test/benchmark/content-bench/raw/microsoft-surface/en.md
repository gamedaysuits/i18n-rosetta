## H3:
How is Surface different from other PCs with built-in AI?
Surface PCs are part of Microsoft’s Copilot PC family—Windows devices designed with built-in AI hardware.
A Surface PC with AI includes the following:
Built-in AI chip (NPU)
Surface Copilot PCs include an integrated Neural Processing Unit (NPU) —a dedicated, on-device AI processor which apps may use to run AI tasks directly. When apps use the NPU to offload and accelerate AI workloads, it can support advanced experiences, like image creation and real-time translation with accelerated speed and efficiency.
New Copilot PC experiences in Windows
On a Surface Copilot PC with AI, you can get Copilot PC experiences in Windows designed to take advantage of that NPU—for example:
Find and re-discover things you’ve seen on your PC more easily with Recall .
Generate and enhance images in Microsoft Photos with Restyle Image , Image Creator , and Super Resolution .
Select text to choose quick, AI-powered actions like summarizing, creating a bulleted list, and rewriting—and much more with Click to Do .
With a Surface PC, enjoy the same Windows desktop, apps, and Surface design you’re used to—but with AI built in.
## H3:
What can I do with AI on a Surface Copilot+ PC?
On a Surface Copilot PC , AI is built-in to help you work, create, and connect. With a Surface Copilot PC, you can do all this and more with AI:
Get writing and research help. Use Copilot on Windows on supported devices to draft or refine emails and documents, summarize content, ask questions, and get ideas in supported apps and services.
Learn more with ease. Use Copilot Vision on supported devices to receive insights and step-by-step guidance based on what Copilot sees on your screen.
Have conversations with AI. Use Copilot Voice on supported devices to talk with Copilot. Just tap or click the microphone on your phone, on the web, or on your laptop or PC to begin a voice chat with Copilot.
Edit and create images. On supported Copilot PCs, Microsoft Photos adds AI features like Restyle Image , Image Creator , and Super Resolution help you enhance or reimagine your photos.
Improve video calls and recordings. On supported Copilot PCs, Windows Studio Effects can adjust framing, blur your background, and enhance audio to help you look and sound your best on calls.
Do more, faster. On supported Copilot PCs, Recall helps you find and jump back into what you’ve already seen on your PC more easily, while Click to Do lets you take faster action to summarize, rewrite, and more.
## H3:
Are AI PCs secure?
Microsoft designs Surface Copilot PCs , with security built in. Protection starts in the hardware and continues through Windows features and ongoing updates. ,
Here’s some of what helps make Surface AI PCs secure:
Hardware-based protection. Surface Copilot AI PCs include the Microsoft Pluton security processor built into the CPU to help protect credentials, identities, and encryption keys.
Security built into Windows 11. Features such as Windows Hello , BitLocker , and Secure Boot help keep your information safer when enabled and kept up to date.
Automatic updates. Both Windows and the Microsoft Pluton security processor receive regular security updates through Windows Update to help maintain protection over time.

## H3:
Which Surface PCs are good for gaming?
Surface PCs run Windows 11, which supports a wide range of PC games, gaming accessories, and cloud gaming services including Xbox Game Pass .
Note: The best Surface device for gaming depends on how you play—whether you’re playing PC games locally, using cloud gaming, or connecting accessories and external monitors for a larger setup.
Here are a few Surface models to consider based on performance capability and flexibility:
Surface Laptop (Copilot PC): Designed for performance and portability, Surface Laptop is well-suited for users who value a portable gaming laptop.
Surface Pro (Copilot PC): Surface Pro ’s flexible design and all-day battery life make it a great 2-in-1 laptop for gaming at home or on the go.
## H3:
How do I choose the best Surface Copilot+ PC for gaming and streaming?
Choosing a Surface Copilot PC for gaming and streaming depends on how you plan to use it—whether you game and stream separately, do both at the same time, or switch between different setups.
When comparing streaming and gaming PCs, consider:
How you plan to use gaming and streaming: Some players stream gameplay live, while others use streaming services to play games, watch content, or game casually. Understanding your usage helps narrow the right balance of performance and flexibility.
Support for accessories and monitors: Both gaming and streaming often involve external monitors, controllers, microphones, webcams, and other accessories . A PC that supports standard peripherals makes it easier to customize or expand your setup.
Connectivity and reliability: Stable connections and modern ports are important for both gaming and streaming, especially when using multiple accessories or external displays.
Flexibility in gaming options: Many players use a mix of local PC gaming and cloud gaming. Choosing a Windows PC that supports both gives you more ways to play and stream content.
Form factor and space: Whether you prefer a portable PC or a more stationary setup can influence what works best for gaming and streaming in your space.
For many users, the best PC for gaming and streaming is one that supports Windows gaming, works with common accessories and monitors, and adapts to different ways of playing and watching. Comparing devices side by side can help you find the option that fits your needs now and as they evolve.
Surface Copilot PCs are designed for productivity and AI-powered experiences, but they can absolutely play games too. Many popular titles run well on Surface, especially with features like Auto Super Resolution that use on-device AI to enhance your gameplay. They're a great fit if you enjoy gaming as part of a well-rounded PC experience — catching up on your favorite games between classes or meetings, playing on the go with outstanding battery life, or streaming games via cloud gaming services like Xbox Cloud Gaming.
